PlayPosit (now WeVideo) allowed our team to embed interactive elements directly into video at precise timestamps — pausing the video to present questions, prompts, and reflections at key learning moments. This turned lecture content into a structured, assessable learning experience. Each "bulb" could include multiple interaction types, a navigable table of contents, closed captions, and transcript access — all within a single viewing environment.
01
Course Navigation Interface — Segmented Lecture with Embedded Interactions
Navigation & Structure
What You're Seeing
A single lecture organized into named segments with interactions at precise timestamps
The left panel shows the full architecture of a single PlayPosit "bulb" — a 1-hour 6-minute lecture broken into clearly labeled video segments. Interactive "Respond" checkpoints appear as distinct items in the navigation, clearly indicating where the video pauses for student engagement. Students can review, take notes, or access a full transcript at any point.
Design Decisions
Segments are named descriptively (e.g., "Systems of Oppression", "The Urgency of Intersectionality") — students can navigate to specific topics for review
Interactions placed at natural content breaks — after foundational concepts and between expert interviews
Review, Notes, and Transcript tabs support multiple learning styles and accessibility needs
Closed captions available throughout — integrated into the production workflow via vendor transcription
02
Embedded Open-Response Interaction — Structural Lens Analysis
Open Response
What You're Seeing
Video paused at a key concept — students respond before continuing
At a critical moment in the lecture on the Structural Lens, the video pauses and presents an open-ended prompt asking students to apply the concept to a real-world conflict of their choosing. Students must identify key actors, power sources, and the role of structural violence before the video resumes. The rich text editor allows formatted, substantive responses.
Design Decisions
Placed at the exact timestamp where the concept was just introduced — ensuring reflection happens while content is fresh
Open-response format chosen deliberately — the complexity of conflict analysis cannot be reduced to multiple choice
Two-part question (A & B) scaffolds thinking from identification to structural analysis
Closed captions (CC) enabled throughout — accessibility built into every interaction
Course instructors could easily generate reports showing each student's response to every interaction — in this course, those responses were used to inform discussion points and tailor content in the weekly live sessions
03
In-Video Graphic — Conflict Cycle Diagram with Active Stage Highlight
In-Video Graphic
What You're Seeing
A custom in-video graphic reinforcing the concept being discussed in real time
As the instructor discusses the "Emergence" stage of the conflict cycle, a custom-designed graphic appears within the video — displaying the full cyclical model with "Emergence" highlighted in the program's accent color. The graphic is designed to appear at the precise moment the concept is named, creating a visual anchor that supports comprehension and retention.
Design Decisions
Graphic timed to appear exactly when the instructor names "Emergence" — synchronizing audio, visual, and text for multi-modal learning
Active stage highlighted in accent color — directing attention without overwhelming the diagram
Full cycle displayed — students see where the active concept sits within the broader model, building systems thinking
Graphic designed by the Multimedia Designer following the course style guide — consistent visual identity throughout
