Week 1 Assignments
Discussion Forum — Introduction to IPC and the Communication Process
Due: Sunday 11:59pm
This post will have three sections: Scenario, Analysis, and Advice.
1
Scenario: Briefly describe an interpersonal exchange that was not effective. You don't have to quote the whole conversation, but you may include some dialogue.
2
Analysis: Use terminology from the communication process — source, receiver, message, encode, decode, channel, feedback, noise — to analyze the exchange.
3
Advice: Give an example of what might have been done differently.
You are also required to provide analysis and/or advice on the posts of two classmates. If two people have already given analysis/advice on a post, choose another.
Sample Student Post (adapted from The Big Bang Theory)
S
Sample Student Post
Original Post
Scenario
I recently went to a wedding with my roommate's girlfriend. She didn't want to go alone, and since he wasn't able to go, he said he didn't mind if I went with her. When I got back, he asked me how it went and I told him I had a really good time. He started to get really upset, and said, "She's not your girlfriend!" I told him that I knew that, and wasn't trying to steal her away from him.
Analysis
As the sender of the message, I thought that my roommate — the receiver — would want to know his girlfriend had fun at the wedding. I thought my message would be what he wanted to hear. I wonder if, when he decoded the message, he was feeling guilty for not going, and so that was psychological noise that led him to misunderstand what I meant when I said we had a good time.
Advice
I think I could have been more sensitive to how he might be feeling, and said something to make him feel better — like, "We had a good time, but you didn't really miss anything."
R
Sample Reply Post
Peer Analysis & Advice
I agree that your roommate could have been feeling guilty, but I also think he might have been jealous. So you could have said, "Well I'm sure she would rather have gone with you, but we still had a good time."
