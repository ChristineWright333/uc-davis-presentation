Part 01 — The Story
Building a Capability
from Scratch
The Starting Point
Joined a team of 7 charged with building SU's first internal online course development capability. Previously outsourced to vendors. Approximately 10 courses built with inconsistent tools and a video production mindset — no unified ID process.
The Mandate
Scale to serve 12 colleges across the entire university. Build a team, a process, and a quality standard that could grow with demand. Serve both continuing education and the broader campus — graduate and undergraduate.
From 10 courses to 300+ — and a landmark national ranking
Over 300 courses across 10 programs (8 & 16-week, UG & Grad), each with async module builds and full LMS development. In 2023, SU ranked #21 in U.S. News Best Online Bachelor's Programs — up from #122 in 2020.

positions gained

Part 02 — My Role
Designing the Workflow
01
First ID on the Team
Brought in to address inconsistent delivery and quality. Applied instructional design standards to a creative, production-driven team environment immediately upon joining.
02
Workflow Architect
Partnered with the Project Manager to design a development workflow: tasks organized by stage, with team member accountability mapped to each task.
03
Faculty Partnership Lead
Collaborated with professors across 12 colleges to extract content, build course maps, and ensure academic integrity within every course design.
The workflow needed to be scalable, trackable, and reportable — while still leaving room for the creativity and innovation that made our team exceptional. Initially drafted in Microsoft Planner, then moved to Trello. Today I've modeled it in a format aligned with Asana, which I understand your team recently adopted.

Part 04 — Quality & Accessibility
Standards Built Into
Every Course
Style Guide System
▶ View Style Guide
  • ✓Program-specific color palettes with full WCAG contrast matrices
  • ✓Typeface selection: Raleway (headings) + Merriweather (body)
  • ✓Style application standards for Rise platform and in-video graphics
  • ✓Color family system — two coordinated families per course
  • ✓Consistent visual brand across all 300+ courses
Accessibility Standards
  • ✓WCAG 2.0 color contrast compliance — all combinations verified
  • ✓Every video captioned — vendor transcription in production timeline
  • ✓All graphics described for screen reader compatibility
  • ✓All tools evaluated for screen reader accessibility before adoption
  • ✓QA accessibility review embedded in final production stage
Accessibility wasn't a checklist — it was a design value that shaped every decision we made, from tool selection to color palette to the way every screen reader would encounter our content.
